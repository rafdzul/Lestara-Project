Southern California Wildfires Fully Contained After 24 Days

February 1, 2025

Two major wildfires, Eaton and Palisades, in Southern California were fully extinguished after burning for 24 days. The fires, fueled by extreme drought and strong winds, destroyed over 37,000 hectares and nearly 16,000 structures, including historic buildings. At least 29 people lost their lives. Heavy rains earlier this week helped firefighters bring the blazes under control.

December 12, 2024

Tropical rainforests, home to over 16,000 species, are rapidly shrinking due to human activities. Only a small portion of intact habitat remains to support endangered wildlife. Protecting these forests is crucial to save biodiversity by 2030.
