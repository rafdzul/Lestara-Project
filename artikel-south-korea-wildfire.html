South Korea’s Largest Wildfire in History, Death Toll Rises to 26

May 27, 2025

A massive wildfire sweeping through southeastern South Korea has claimed at least 26 lives, making it the deadliest and largest wildfire in the country’s history. The blaze, fueled by strong winds and prolonged dry weather, has forced over 40,000 residents to evacuate their homes.

Emergency crews have been working around the clock to contain the fire, but rapidly shifting winds continue to spread the flames across towns and forests. The government has declared a national state of emergency, deploying military forces to assist in firefighting and evacuation efforts.

Officials warn that the wildfire’s unprecedented scale may be a result of worsening climate conditions. As recovery operations begin, South Korea braces for the long-term environmental and economic impacts of this historic disaster.
